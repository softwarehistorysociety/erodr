erodr is a private mobile social networking app for the local college student community.

It creates a geo-fenced social media Forum around the local campus. For the first time since Facebook was college-students only, erodr provides an open social platform for the student community that is private from the rest of the world. On erodr you can meet new students around you, such a compelling interest that typical erodr users refresh their streamer > 45X per day to see who and what's coming next...

You register using your student email address.

erodr is different from other social media, your posts are viewed in an open forum that reaches other students not already in your other social networks.  Your posts collect private likes and comments, remarks seen only by you, which is how other students introduce themselves if interested. Simply request to Connect to enable 1:1 messaging.

Privacy controls make it more comfortable to post in the open forum.  You are invisible until you like other's posts, comment, or post something yourself. All posts to the erodr streamer expire in hours.  Well-liked posts can last for up to 5 days it they have no hard limit set. Users optionally set hard limits on duration (as little as 30 minutes), range (down to 100 feet), and audience (public, connections only, friends only, men or women only). Users can post anonymously once a day.

erodr is community moderated: likes, dislikes, and flags determine each post's duration and viewable range. Posts can expire in minutes if disliked by other students.

erodr is an addictive hyperlocal crowd-sourced entertainment feed where the student community mingles and meets.
