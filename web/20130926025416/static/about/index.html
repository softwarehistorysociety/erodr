How it all started... erodr was the brainchild of Drew Halliday, then a senior at University of Missouri, Columbia (Mizzou). In early 2012 he began to develop prototypes for a student-designed local social network with better privacy than other social media. Importantly, this network would create a community of students around the campus where users could meet people not already friends in their other social media.  Version 1 was launched on the App Store on November 18, 2012, in a closed beta at Mizzou. In February 2013 Spring Semester 4 other schools (Temple, Virginia Tech, IUP, and Bloomsburg) were added. At Mizzou, about 3,000 students actively used erodr as their online social media over the summer break. Now the company is launching 40 new campuses for Fall Semester 2013.
